Objectives & Overview

Overview

This lesson introduces children to the magnificent story of creation. Through engaging activities, storytelling, and hands-on experiences, students will explore the seven days of creation and understand God's intentional design of the world. They will discover their special role as caretakers of God's creation and learn to appreciate the beauty and wonder around them.

Learning Objectives

  • Students will understand the sequence of the seven days of creation
  • Students will recognize that God created everything with purpose and intention
  • Students will identify ways they can care for God's creation
  • Students will develop a sense of wonder and gratitude for the world God made

Lesson Content

Lesson Outline

Opening Prayer & Welcome (5 minutes)

Begin the lesson by welcoming your students warmly. Invite them to gather together and start with a prayer, specifically thanking God for His amazing creation and the beautiful world He has given us.

Introduction Activity (5 minutes)

Engage the children with a "Creation Guessing Game." Show picture cards displaying different parts of creation‚Äîsuch as the sun, animals, plants, and stars‚Äîand challenge the students to guess which day of creation each item belongs to. This fun activity builds anticipation for the lesson and gets them thinking about the order of creation.

Bible Story - The Seven Days (15 minutes)

Read Genesis 1:1-31 using an age-appropriate Bible or storybook. As you read, encourage students to actively participate by acting out different aspects of creation, such as making waves for the sea or flying like birds. Walk them through the seven days: on Day 1, God created light and separated it from darkness. Day 2 brought the sky and waters. On Day 3, land, seas, and plants appeared. Day 4 saw the creation of the sun, moon, and stars. Fish and birds filled the world on Day 5, followed by land animals and people on Day 6. Finally, on Day 7, God rested.

Interactive Activity - Creation Collage (15 minutes)

Validate their learning with a hands-on "Seven Days of Creation" accordion book project. Have students fold paper into seven sections and draw or glue pictures representing each day's creation. This activity reinforces the sequence and details of the creation story in a creative and memorable way.

Application & Discussion (5 minutes)

Lead a discussion on how we can take care of God's creation. Ask the students, "What are some ways we can show respect for the world God made?" You can list their responses on chart paper, encouraging ideas like recycling, not littering, being kind to animals, and planting trees.

Closing Prayer & Memory Verse (5 minutes)

Conclude the lesson by practicing the memory verse, Genesis 1:1: "In the beginning, God created the heavens and the earth." You can use hand motions to help them remember it better. Close with a prayer thanking God for His wonderful creation and asking Him to help us be good caretakers of His world.

Discussion Questions

Q: What was your favorite day of creation? Why?
Q: Why do you think God created so many different kinds of animals and plants?
Q: What does it mean that God created us "in His image"?
Q: How does learning about creation help us understand who God is?
Q: What's one way you can take care of God's creation this week?
