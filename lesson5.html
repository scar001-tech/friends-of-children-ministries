Noah's Ark Adventure

Scripture: Genesis 6-9 Date: November 3, 2025

Lesson Sections

Objectives & Overview Lesson Content Discussions Media Resources

Objectives & Overview

Overview

This engaging lesson brings the beloved story of Noah's Ark to life! Through fun animal activities, interactive songs, and creative crafts, participants will discover important truths about obedience, God's promises, and His care for all creation. The rainbow covenant reminds us that God always keeps His promises.

Learning Objectives

  • Learn the story of Noah and the great flood
  • Understand the importance of obeying God
  • Recognize that God keeps His promises
  • Identify the rainbow as a sign of God's covenant
  • Appreciate God's care for all His creation

Lesson Content

Lesson Outline

Welcome & Animal Sounds Game (5 minutes)

Kick off the lesson with high energy by playing the "Animal Sounds" game! Call out names of different animals‚Äîlike lions, elephants, birds, and sheep‚Äîand have the children respond with the appropriate sounds and motions. This icebreaker gets everyone moving, laughing, and ready for the animal-themed lesson.

Story Time - Noah's Big Boat (12 minutes)

Capture their imagination by telling the story of Noah's Ark. Explain the problem: people had stopped listening to God. Describe God's plan to have Noah build a giant boat, and Noah's faithful obedience even when it seemed silly. Share how two of every kind of animal marched onto the ark, how the rain fell for 40 days and nights, and how God remembered them, eventually sending a rainbow as a promise never to flood the whole earth again. Use toy animals or pictures to make the story come alive!

Animal March Activity (5 minutes)

Play the song "The Animals Went In Two by Two" and have the children march around the room in pairs. Encourage them to pretend to be different animals boarding the ark. They can hop like bunnies, stomp like elephants, slither like snakes, or fly like birds, reinforcing the story through kinetic learning.

Craft Time - Rainbow Promise Craft (15 minutes)

Create a colorful "Rainbow Promise" craft to remind them of God's faithfulness. Using blue construction paper as the sky, have students glue cotton balls for clouds and use paint, crayons, or tissue paper to create a vibrant rainbow. Add animal stickers and write "God keeps His promises!" at the bottom. Discuss the colors of the rainbow as they work.

Discussion - Obeying God (5 minutes)

Settle down for a brief discussion about obedience. Ask simple questions like, "What did God ask Noah to do?" and "Did Noah obey?" Talk about what the rainbow reminds us of and brainstorm simple ways we can obey God in our own lives today.

Closing Song & Prayer (3 minutes)

End the lesson by singing "Arky, Arky" or "I Can Obey." Close with a simple prayer thanking God for the story of Noah and for keeping His promises, asking Him to help us be obedient just like Noah was.

Discussion Questions

Q: Why did God ask Noah to build an ark?
Q: What animals would you want to see on the ark?
Q: What does it mean that God "keeps His promises"?
Q: When you see a rainbow, what should you remember?
