The Good Samaritan

Scripture: Luke 10:25-37 Date: November 10, 2025

Lesson Sections

Objectives & Overview Lesson Content Discussions Media Resources

Objectives & Overview

Overview

This powerful lesson introduces Jesus' parable of the Good Samaritan, teaching about compassion, kindness, and loving our neighbors. Through interactive role-play, thoughtful discussions, and practical applications, participants will learn to show Christ-like love to everyone, regardless of differences. The lesson emphasizes that our "neighbor" is anyone in need.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the meaning of Jesus' parable of the Good Samaritan
  • Learn who our "neighbor" is according to Jesus
  • Identify practical ways to show kindness and compassion
  • Recognize that loving others means taking action
  • Break down barriers and prejudices that prevent us from helping

Lesson Content

Lesson Outline

Opening Discussion (5 minutes)

Start with a thought-provoking question: "Who is your neighbor?" Allow the participants to share their initial thoughts and list their responses on chart paper. Explain that today's lesson will expand our understanding of who our neighbor really is, going beyond just the people who live next door.

Bible Story - The Good Samaritan (10 minutes)

Read or dramatically tell the parable from Luke 10:25-37. Recount the story of a man who was attacked by robbers while traveling. Describe how a priest and a Levite both saw him but chose to walk by on the other side. Then, introduce the Samaritan who not only stopped but helped and cared for the injured man. Conclude with Jesus' powerful question: "Which was a neighbor to the hurt man?" Provide historical context by explaining that Jews and Samaritans were enemies, making the Samaritan's actions even more significant.

Interactive Role-Play (15 minutes)

Divide the class into groups to act out the parable. Assign roles such as the Narrator, Injured Man, Priest, Levite, and Good Samaritan. After the role-play, facilitate a discussion about how it felt to be each character. Ask questions like, "Why did some pass by?" and "What made the Samaritan different?" to encourage empathy and understanding.

Modern-Day Application (10 minutes)

Connect the story to their lives by presenting real-life scenarios where they can be "Good Samaritans." Discuss situations like seeing someone sitting alone at lunch, helping a classmate who dropped their books, stepping in when someone is being bullied, or assisting an elderly neighbor with groceries. Ask the group what a "Good Samaritan" would do in each instance and discuss what fear or hesitation might stop us from helping.

Kindness Challenge Activity (10 minutes)

Create "Kindness Challenge Cards" where each person writes or draws one specific act of kindness they commit to doing this week. Ideas can include helping with homework, sharing a kind word, including someone who is left out, or doing a chore without being asked. Have them decorate their cards with stickers and share their commitment with the group to build accountability.

Closing Prayer & Commissioning (5 minutes)

Finish with the memory verse from Luke 10:27: "Love your neighbor as yourself." Close with a prayer, asking God to open our eyes to opportunities to be Good Samaritans and to give us the courage to act with compassion towards everyone we meet.

Discussion Questions

Q: Why do you think the priest and Levite didn't help?
Q: What made the Samaritan's help so special and surprising?
Q: Who is it hardest for you to show kindness to? Why?
Q: Can you think of a time when someone was a "Good Samaritan" to you?
Q: What stops us from helping others even when we know we should?
