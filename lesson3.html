Living with Purpose

Scripture: Jeremiah 29:11 Date: November 17, 2025

Lesson Sections

Objectives & Overview Lesson Content Discussions Media Resources

Objectives & Overview

Overview

This transformative lesson helps participants discover God's unique plan for their lives. Through engaging discussions, personal reflection, and practical applications, students will explore their God-given gifts, passions, and calling. They'll learn how to live intentionally for Christ in every area of their lives.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand that God has a unique purpose for each person
  • Identify personal gifts, talents, and passions
  • Learn to seek God's guidance in decision-making
  • Develop a vision for living purposefully for Christ
  • Create actionable steps to pursue God's calling

Lesson Content

Lesson Outline

Opening Activity - "My Strengths" (10 minutes)

Begin with a self-reflection exercise to help students identify their unique qualities. Have each person write down three things they are good at, two things they are passionate about, and one way they want to make a difference in the world. Afterward, facilitate sharing in pairs or small groups to encourage openness and mutual appreciation.

Scripture Study (10 minutes)

Read Jeremiah 29:11 together: "For I know the plans I have for you," declares the Lord, "plans to prosper you and not to harm you, plans to give you hope and a future." Discuss what it means that God has specific plans for us, how knowing His purpose brings hope, and the difference between God's perfect plans and our own often limited understanding.

Teaching - Finding Your Purpose (15 minutes)

Teach the key principles of discovering God's purpose. Explain that we should pray for guidance daily, identify the gifts God has given us, and look for opportunities to serve others. Emphasize that we must trust the journey as God reveals His purpose over time, and stay grounded in His Word to guide our path. Share inspiring real-life testimonies or video clips of people who discovered their calling.

Interactive Activity - Purpose Vision Board (10 minutes)

Guide participants in creating a simple vision board. Ask them to outline their God-given gifts and talents, identify areas where they feel called to serve, set short-term goals for the current month or year, and visualize a long-term vision for their future. Encourage them to include Scripture verses that inspire and motivate them.

Small Group Discussion (10 minutes)

Break into small groups for a deeper discussion. Explore questions such as what obstacles might prevent them from living purposefully, how they can start pursuing God's purpose this week, and identifying who can support and encourage them on this journey.

Closing & Commitment (5 minutes)

Close the session with a commitment exercise. Ask each person to write down one specific action they will take this week to pursue God's purpose, such as volunteering, developing a skill, or spending time in prayer. End with a group prayer, asking God to reveal His specific purpose to each person.

Discussion Questions

Q: What do you think God's purpose for your life might be?
Q: How have you seen God working in your life already?
Q: What fears or doubts hold you back from fully pursuing God's plan?
Q: Who is someone whose purposeful life inspires you?
Q: How can living with purpose impact those around you?
